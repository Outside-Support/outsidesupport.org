Online · For Middle Schoolers

Your child doesn't have to face bullying alone.

Outside Support runs a six-week online support group for middle schoolers experiencing bullying. Students are never placed with anyone from their own school — giving them a safe space to connect with peers who understand, while learning problem-solving and coping strategies.

Bullying leaves a mark that doesn't disappear when school ends.

Most students who experience bullying suffer in silence. Outside Support exists to change that. Our program gives students the skills, language, and peer connections they need to feel empowered and move forward.

The BELONG Program

Six weeks that can change how your child sees themselves.

Developed and piloted with bullying expert Dr. Sue Swearer at the University of Nebraska, BELONG is an evidence-based, facilitator-led curriculum in skill building and peer support, designed specifically for middle schoolers who've experienced bullying. It is built around three core outcomes.

🔍
Build Understanding

Knowledge is power. Students learn to identify what bullying is, why it happens, and — critically — that it is never their fault. Naming the experience is the first step to moving past it.

🧠
Develop Skills

Each session introduces practical, age-appropriate tools: problem-solving skills, coping strategies for hard moments, and ways to ask for help with confidence.

🤝
Create Connections

Students discover they are not alone. Small facilitated groups create space for real peer connections — the kind that come from shared experience and genuine understanding. And because the program is online, we guarantee no student is ever grouped with someone from their own school.
