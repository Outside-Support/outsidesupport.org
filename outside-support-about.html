About Us

We exist for the kid who's going through it right now.

Outside Support was founded on a simple belief: every middle schooler who experiences bullying deserves real skills, real support, and peers who get what they're going through — regardless of where they live or what their family can afford.

Our Mission
Equip every student who's experienced bullying with the skills and connections to move forward.

Bullying affects one in five students — and the impact doesn't end when the school day does. Most existing support focuses on preventing bullying or addressing the behavior of those who bully. Outside Support takes a different approach: we focus on the student who was targeted, giving them a structured, facilitator-led experience built around social-emotional skills, peer connection, and practical tools for navigating what comes next.

Our BELONG Program is grounded in over two decades of bullying research from the BRAVE Lab at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. It's not therapy. It's a skill-building program — designed to be accessible, evidence-based, and free for every student who needs it.

Our Foundation

Built on decades of research.

Outside Support didn't start from scratch. Our BELONG Program is directly grounded in the work of the BRAVE Lab at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln — one of the longest-running bullying research programs in the country.

The BELONG program, piloted at the University of Nebraska, served as the direct research foundation for Outside Support. Its curriculum, facilitator approach, and peer group model were developed and tested with real middle schoolers — and the results informed everything we built.

When we say evidence-based, we mean it. Not just inspired by research — built from it.
