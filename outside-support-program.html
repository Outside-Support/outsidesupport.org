The BELONG Program

The only program of its kind — designed specifically for middle schoolers who've experienced bullying, built around the science of what actually helps.

Over six weeks, small groups of middle schoolers meet online with a trained facilitator to build understanding, develop social-emotional skills, and find their footing again through peer connection.

What We Cover

Three areas that make a real difference.

The BELONG Program is an evidence-based, facilitator-led curriculum in social-emotional skills and peer support. Every topic is grounded in research and designed specifically for the middle school experience — all explored in a small group setting with peers who truly understand what they're going through.

01
Understanding Bullying

Students develop a clear, grounded understanding of what bullying actually is — and what it isn't. This shared language helps them make sense of their own experiences without self-blame, and gives them the words to talk about what's happening — with a parent, a trusted adult, or anyone they choose to tell.

Bullying vs. teasing Types of bullying Bullying behaviors Why kids bully
02
Problem Solving

Students practice real-world strategies for navigating bullying situations — including how to respond in the moment and how to handle bullying within their own friend group, which is often the hardest to address.

What to do in the moment Example scenarios How to respond Peer group dynamics
03
Build Resilience

Students learn to recognize the connection between how they think, how they feel, and how they act — and build the confidence and inner strength to move forward. This is about developing a stronger sense of self that extends well beyond the program.

Coping strategies Building confidence Emotional awareness Moving forward

Our Guarantee
No student is ever grouped with someone from their own school.

Because the program is fully online, we can draw students from across the country — which means every group is made up of peers who share the same experience, but not the same hallways. Kids open up when they know no one from school will find out.

For Parents & Caregivers

Getting started is simple.

A straightforward path to something that can really help. We take the time to make sure the program is the right fit for your child before they join a group.

1
Refer your child

Fill out our referral form to get started. Parent or guardian contact information is required to make a referral.

2
Complete consent forms

If your child is a good fit for the program, we'll send over consent forms for you to complete. Parent or guardian consent and student assent are obtained before participation begins.

3
Get placed in a group

Your child is matched to a small group of peers and the program begins — online, on a schedule that works for your family, completely free.

For Schools & Counselors

A free resource your students can count on.

Outside Support is designed to work alongside your existing programs — not replace them. Referring a student takes minutes, and there's no cost to your school, ever.

✓
Evidence-based curriculum Grounded in peer-reviewed research from the BRAVE Lab at the University of Nebraska. Not a one-size-fits-all program — built specifically for middle schoolers who've experienced bullying.
✓
Free to refer There are no fees, no contracts, and no administrative burden for your school.
✓
Trained facilitators lead every session Every group is led by a facilitator trained in the BELONG Program curriculum, so the quality of each student's experience is consistent and intentional.
✓
A complement to what you already do Outside Support works best when paired with school-based support. We're an outside resource — not a replacement for the trusted adults already in your students' lives.
